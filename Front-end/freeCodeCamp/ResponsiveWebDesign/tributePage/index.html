Sundar Pichai

Pioneering Progress in the World of Technology

Here's a time line of Sundar Pichai's life:

  • 1972: Sundar Pichai is born on July 12 in Madurai, Tamil Nadu, India.
  • 1993-1997: Sundar completes his Bachelor of Technology in Metallurgical Engineering from the Indian Institute of Technology (IIT) Kharagpur, one of India's premier engineering institutions.
  • 1997-2002: He earns a Master of Science in Material Sciences and Engineering from Stanford University and later completes his MBA from the Wharton School at the University of Pennsylvania.
  • 2004: Sundar joins Google as a product manager. In his early years at Google, he works on the Google Toolbar and other browser products.
  • 2008: Pichai becomes responsible for overseeing the development of Google Chrome, a web browser launched by Google. Chrome quickly becomes one of the most popular web browsers globally.
  • 2012: Sundar Pichai is promoted to Senior Vice President at Google and takes charge of Chrome and Apps. This includes overseeing Google Drive, Gmail, and other cloud-based products.
  • 2013: Pichai's responsibilities expand further as he takes over Android, the world's most widely used mobile operating system.
  • 2015: In a significant career move, Sundar Pichai is named the CEO of Google Inc. The restructuring of Google into Alphabet Inc. sees Google's co-founders Larry Page and Sergey Brin focusing on the broader goals of Alphabet, while Pichai takes the helm at Google.
  • 2016: As CEO of Google, Pichai oversees various projects, including the development of the Google Assistant, Google Home, and Pixel smartphones
  • 2018: Google announces a $1 billion investment in a new campus in New York City, signaling its commitment to expanding its presence in the tech industry under Pichai's leadership.
  • 2019: Sundar Pichai takes on additional responsibilities as the CEO of Alphabet Inc. This appointment makes him one of the most influential figures in the tech industry, overseeing both Google and Alphabet's diverse range of projects, including Waymo (self-driving cars) and Verily (life sciences).
  • 2020: Under Pichai's leadership, Google and Alphabet continue to innovate in areas such as artificial intelligence, cloud computing, and quantum computing.
